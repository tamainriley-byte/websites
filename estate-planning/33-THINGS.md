# 33-THINGS.md — "33 Things To Do Before You Die"

The AI-video explainer series. One thing per video, 30–45 seconds, numbered like a
countdown so people binge the set. Each item maps to an Estate Score pillar and a
service page (same slug), so the series *is* the SEO architecture.

Format per clip: bold number card ("№ 7 / 33") → one-line hook → 3 beats of
explanation (animated graphics / AI presenter / adviser VO over b-roll) → "That's
one more thing sorted." → Estate Score CTA. Full production spec in PRODUCTION.md.

Series framing (say it in clip 1): *"Not a bucket list. The other list — the one
that makes everything easier for the people you love. Most take under an hour."*

---

## The list (with hooks)

**Pillar: Will & Wishes**
1. **Write a will.** Hook: "Die without one and the government decides who gets everything — and it's probably not who you think."
2. **Update the will after every big life event.** "Married? Divorced? New baby? Your old will might now be dangerously wrong. Marriage can cancel it entirely."
3. **Write an expression of wishes.** "The will says who gets what. This letter says *why* — and it's the page that stops the family falling out."
4. **Choose your executors on purpose.** "The default choice — your eldest — is often the worst one. Here's how to pick."
5. **Record your funeral wishes.** "Party or quiet? Buried, cremated, fired into space? If it's not written down, your family has to guess — while grieving."
6. **Write the letters.** "A sealed letter for each grandchild's 18th birthday costs nothing and outlives everything else you own."
7. **Decide about the specific things.** "The ring. The watch. The recipe book. Small items cause the biggest wars — name them."

**Pillar: Protection**
8. **Set up a health & welfare LPA.** "If you can't speak for yourself, who tells the doctors what you'd want? Right now, legally: maybe nobody."
9. **Set up a property & finance LPA.** "Your spouse can't automatically run your accounts if you lose capacity. Even joint ones can be frozen."
10. **Name guardians for your kids.** "If it's not written down, a court decides who raises your children. One page fixes it."
11. **Put life insurance in trust.** "One form, usually free, and the payout skips probate and possibly tax. Most policies never have it done."
12. **Check your cover actually fits your life.** "That policy from 2011? It doesn't know about your second kid or your bigger mortgage."
13. **Build the 'if I'm hit by a bus' file.** "One place your family can find everything. We'll show you what goes in it."

**Pillar: Money**
14. **Find your lost pensions.** "The average worker has 11 jobs and loses track of billions in pensions. Some of it might be yours."
15. **Do your pension nominations.** "Your pension usually ISN'T covered by your will. Whoever's on the nomination form gets it — is that still the right person?"
16. **Get one picture of everything.** "Accounts, pots, policies — on one page. Most couples have never seen their whole position once."
17. **Run the retirement maths.** "Most people have never actually done the sum. Some find out they can retire years earlier."
18. **Keep an emergency fund.** "Three to six months of calm, in cash. It's the foundation everything else stands on."
19. **Talk to your partner about money. Properly. Once.** "Thirty years married, never once discussed it — more common than you'd believe."

**Pillar: Tax & Estate**
20. **Find out if inheritance tax even applies to you.** "Most families worry about it needlessly — and some who should worry, don't. Find out which you are in five minutes."
21. **Use your gifting allowances.** "There are amounts you can give away every single year, tax-free. Miss a year, lose it forever."
22. **Consider giving while living.** "The deposit, the driving lessons, the trip — while you're there to see their face. Often smarter tax-wise, too."
23. **Get the family home properly reviewed.** "There are extra allowances around passing on your home. Set up wrong, you can lose them."
24. **Ask whether a trust fits.** "Not just for the rich — protecting a vulnerable child, a second marriage, or a family cottage. Ask the question."
25. **Plan the business succession.** "If you run a business: what happens to it the day you don't? Your foreman, your family and the taxman all need to know."
26. **Equalise between the kids — on paper.** "You helped one child more? Everyone remembers. Write down how it balances out, and nobody falls out."

**Pillar: Modern Life**
27. **Make a digital assets plan.** "Thirty thousand photos, all behind your face ID. When you're gone, so are they — unless you plan it."
28. **List your accounts and subscriptions.** "Give your family the map: every account, policy and login location on one sheet."
29. **Sort the photos while you can caption them.** "Print the best 200. Your grandkids won't scroll your cloud, but they'll keep an album."
30. **Record your story.** "One hour of you on video, telling the family stories. The single most-treasured thing you can leave."
31. **Tell people where everything is.** "The best plan in the world fails if it's a secret. Have the 20-minute conversation."
32. **Put a yearly review in the calendar.** "Life changes, plans rot. One re-check a year keeps your score real."
33. **Check your Estate Score.** "Five minutes, 25 questions, one number that tells you which of these 32 you've actually done. Start here — or finish here. Link in bio."

## Production notes

- **Batching:** these need no adviser time — script + AI voice/presenter + template
  graphics. Produce in batches of 10; the whole series can be live in a month, then
  loop remixes (new hooks, seasonal cuts: "3 things to sort before the school year",
  "the January list").
- **Numbering builds the binge:** always show "№ x / 33"; pin a playlist/highlight
  with all of them in order. Long-form YouTube cut: "All 33 things in 12 minutes."
- **Each item = a web page** at `/things/nn-slug` with the video embedded,
  `HowTo` + `VideoObject` + `FAQPage` schema, and the Estate Score CTA — 33 pages of
  long-tail SEO matching exactly what people search ("do I need a power of attorney",
  "what happens if I die without a will").
- **Compliance:** items 11–26 brush regulated territory — keep clips generic
  ("ask whether", "find out if"), never prescriptive; IFA-pillar clips go through the
  same sign-off step as adviser videos.
- The list is also the lead magnet PDF ("The 33 Things checklist") for email capture,
  and the spine of the PPC landing pages.
