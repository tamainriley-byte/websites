# SCRIPTS.md — The Client Story Script Bank

This is the exact voice: warm, off-the-cuff, like texting a friend after work. The
paperwork is never the point. The point is **The Thing** — the human moment.

---

## 1. The formula (every Client Story follows it)

1. **Warm open (2s):** "Hey — I just wanted to share something from today."
2. **Setup (5–10s):** who you met (anonymised: "a lovely couple", "a widow in her
   70s", "a chap who runs a family business") and the situation — often "they hadn't
   really been looked after by their existing advisers, so we're going to be a fresh
   pair of eyes on their estate planning."
3. **The pivot (the signature line, 5s):** "And look — we can sort all of their
   affairs out. The will, the powers of attorney, the tax. **But that's not the
   thing. The thing was…**"
4. **The Thing (15–30s):** the human moment. What the money or planning is *really*
   for. Told with genuine feeling.
5. **The close (5–10s):** how it felt. "I think they were really moved." /
   "This is why I love this job."
6. **Sign-off (always):** *"Another family, sorted."* (+ soft CTA in caption:
   "Check your Estate Score — free, 5 minutes, link in bio.")

### Rules
- **One story per video.** Don't stack two.
- **Anonymise and blend.** No names, change ages/places/family shapes. If it could
  only be one client, it's not usable.
- **No advice, no numbers-promises.** "We can often save tax" not "we saved £40k".
  (IFA clips go through compliance sign-off — see AUTOMATION.md.)
- **Talk, don't present.** Film it like a voice note. First take is usually the best.
- Rotate the openers and closers below so 17 people don't sound like a script.

### Opener variations
- "Hey — I just wanted to share, I had such a lovely meeting today."
- "Okay, I have to tell you about today."
- "Just walked out of a client meeting and I'm still smiling."
- "So this is why I do this job. Quick story."
- "You know what happened today? Something really special, actually."

### Closer variations
- "I think they were genuinely moved. I know I was."
- "They walked out lighter. You could see it."
- "Honestly, best part of my week."
- "And that's the bit the paperwork never shows you."
- "Twenty minutes of moving paper around, and that's what it bought them."

## 2. Estate Planner scripts (30)

> Each is written to be spoken naturally in 45–75 seconds. Advisers should make it
> their own — same shape, their words.

**EP-01 — The quarterly family meal (the flagship — film this one first)**
"Hey — I just wanted to share, I had such a lovely meeting today. Met a potentially
new client, and honestly, they hadn't really been looked after by their existing
advisers, so we're going to be a fresh pair of eyes on their estate planning and
financial needs. And we can sort all of their affairs out — but that's not the thing.
The thing was, when we found the tax they didn't need to be paying, they didn't know
what to do with it. So I made a suggestion, and they loved it: we're setting up —
literally by moving some paper around — a quarterly meal for the whole family to meet
and catch up. They get to bring the family together four times a year, *and* save
some tax. I think they were really moved. Another family, sorted."

**EP-02 — The letter for the 18th birthdays**
"Quick story from today. Sat with a grandmother, mid-70s, sorting her will — which we
did, that's the easy bit. But that's not the thing. The thing was, she has six
grandchildren, and alongside the will we're putting away a sealed letter for each of
them, to be opened on their 18th birthday, with a little gift attached. She said, 'I
might not be at all of those birthdays — but now I'll be at every one of them.' I had
to hold it together, honestly. Another family, sorted."

**EP-03 — The fishing trip fund**
"So this is why I do this job. Chap came in to update his will, and we got talking
about his sons — they only ever really see each other at Christmas. So inside the
estate plan we set up a little annual pot: it pays for a fishing weekend, every year,
for the brothers to go together. His words: 'I don't want to leave them money, I want
to leave them each other.' Come on. Another family, sorted."

**EP-04 — Giving while living**
"Just walked out of a meeting and I'm still smiling. Lovely couple, worried about
inheritance tax, thought the answer was some complicated trust. And look, we can sort
all of that. But the thing was — when we looked properly, they could safely gift their
daughter her house deposit *now*. Not in twenty years. Now. So instead of her
inheriting it one day, they get to stand in the hallway of her first home. They'd just
never had anyone show them they could afford to. Another family, sorted."

**EP-05 — The blended family peace deal**
"Okay, honest one today. Met a couple, second marriage, kids on both sides, and
they'd been avoiding the will conversation for *years* because they were scared it
would cause a row. We sorted it in one afternoon — everyone protected, everyone
treated fairly, all written down. But that's not the thing. The thing was the silence
in the room afterwards. She said, 'That's the first time in ten years I haven't been
worried about this.' That's not paperwork, that's peace. Another family, sorted."

**EP-06 — The recipe book**
"You know what happened today? Sorting an estate plan for a lady who's cooked Sunday
lunch for her family for fifty years. Wills, powers of attorney, all done — fine.
But the thing was, she kept saying 'the recipes are all in my head.' So part of her
plan now is a properly printed family recipe book, one copy for each child and
grandchild, paid for and organised through the estate. She said that book *is* the
inheritance. Another family, sorted."

**EP-07 — The empty chair**
"Quick one, and it got me today. A widower, updating everything after losing his
wife. We sorted the lot — but the thing was, he told me their family does an 'empty
chair' dinner on her birthday every year, and he was worried it would stop when he
goes. So it's now written into his wishes, with a small fund attached: the dinner
carries on, chair and all. He said, 'Now they'll keep both of us at the table.'
Another family, sorted."

**EP-08 — The neighbour and the allotment**
"Had such a lovely meeting today. Gentleman in his 80s, no children, and his old
advisers had basically copy-pasted his will. Fresh pair of eyes, and I just asked:
'Who's actually been there for you?' And he lit up talking about his neighbour — 
twenty years of lifts to hospital, tomatoes over the fence. So the allotment, and a
proper thank-you, now go to the neighbour. He said, 'I never knew I was allowed to do
that.' You're allowed. It's your story. Another family, sorted."

**EP-09 — The funeral that's a party**
"So today was different. Client said the thing she was most scared of wasn't dying —
it was everyone being miserable at her funeral. So along with the will we wrote her
send-off plan: the playlist — there's ABBA on it, obviously — the food, a toast with
her favourite wine, all funded and written down so nobody has to guess. She left the
office laughing about her own funeral. That's the job done right. Another family,
sorted."

**EP-10 — The driving lessons**
"Just a small one today, but it's my favourite kind. Grandparents, three teenage
grandkids, wanted to 'leave them something meaningful' — and we worked out that with
some simple gifting, they could pay for all three lots of driving lessons *now*, tax
efficiently. So instead of being a line in a will one day, grandad gets three phone
calls when they pass their tests. He was chuffed to bits. Another family, sorted."

**EP-11 — The business handover**
"Okay, I have to tell you about today. Met a man who's run the same family firm for
thirty years, and his estate plan — from his old advisers — didn't even mention the
business. The thing was his foreman: twenty-two years of service, basically family.
So the succession plan now includes a route for him to take a stake and keep the
name over the door. Client said, 'I'm not leaving a company, I'm leaving a promise.'
Another family, sorted."

**EP-12 — The first passports**
"Lovely one today. A grandmother whose family has never been abroad together — ever.
When we tidied up her affairs and found what she could comfortably gift, she didn't
want anything for herself. So this summer she's paying for the first passports and
one big family holiday, while she's there to be in the photos. 'Better than flowers
on a grave,' she said. She's not wrong. Another family, sorted."

**EP-13 — Nobody had ever asked**
"This one stopped me today. Reviewing a will an old firm had done — technically fine,
completely soulless. So I just asked the client: 'What do you actually want to
*happen*?' And she went quiet and said, 'No one's ever asked me that.' Forty minutes
later we had it all — the house, the charity she's loved for years, the ring that
goes to the granddaughter named after her. Same documents. Completely different
story. Another family, sorted."

**EP-14 — The dog**
"Quick story, because people always laugh and then go quiet. Client's biggest worry
wasn't the house or the money — it was Alfie, her spaniel. So her plan now names who
takes him, with a small fund for food and vet bills. It took ten minutes. She said
it's the first night in a year she'll sleep properly. Never underestimate what
'sorted' means to someone. Another family — and Alfie — sorted."

**EP-15 — The scholarship**
"So this happened today. A retired teacher, modest estate, kept apologising that it
'wasn't much'. But the thing was — we set up a small annual prize at the school she
taught at for forty years. Book money for a kid who tries hard. It'll run for
decades. She cried, I nearly did, and it cost her less than she expected. There's no
such thing as a small estate — just unfinished stories. Another family, sorted."

**EP-16 — The school trips**
"Had a lovely couple in today. Their grandkids keep missing school trips because
money's tight for their son and his wife — and they'd been quietly heartbroken about
it. Turns out, with the gifting allowances they'd never used, every school trip is
now covered, every year. The thing is: nobody has to have an awkward conversation.
It's just… handled. Grandma's the hero forever. Another family, sorted."

**EP-17 — The family history video**
"Today's plan came with homework — for me. Client in his 80s, amazing life, navy then
forty years on the railways. His kids know maybe a tenth of it. So alongside the will,
we're arranging a filmed sit-down — him telling his stories, properly recorded, a copy
for each child. He said, 'So the great-grandkids know I wasn't always old.' I love
this job. Another family, sorted."

**EP-18 — The two sisters**
"Honest one today. Two sisters fell out over their mum's estate — because there was
no will, and everyone guessed differently about what she'd have wanted. We couldn't
fix that. But their brother came to us so it never happens to *his* kids: everything
written, everything explained, even a letter saying *why*. The thing about a will
isn't the money. It's that nobody has to guess. Another family, sorted."

**EP-19 — The carer**
"This one's special. A gentleman whose carer has looked after him for six years —
holidays, birthdays, hospital runs. His old will didn't mention her; he'd assumed he
couldn't. Done properly, documented properly, he can — and now she's remembered in
it, fairly, alongside his family who all agreed she'd earned it. He said, 'Now it
says thank you even when I can't.' Another family, sorted."

**EP-20 — Skiing at eighty**
"Best meeting of the month. A widow, 79, super sharp, and her plan was all about
being careful. And when we actually ran through everything, the thing was — she can
afford to *live*. So item one on her new estate plan, before the trusts and the tax:
she's booked the ski trip she's talked about for twenty years. Estate planning isn't
only about after. It's about permission to enjoy now. Another family, sorted."

**EP-21 — The house that stays a home**
"Quick story. A couple's biggest fear was the family cottage — three generations of
summers — being sold off in a rush one day. So we've structured it so the kids can
keep it, share it, and there's a pot for the roof and the bills so nobody resents it.
The thing was watching them realise the arguments they'd imagined just… won't happen.
They planned the next summer there before they left the office. Another family,
sorted."

**EP-22 — The godson**
"Today reminded me why fresh eyes matter. New client, no children, and her old will —
done years ago — left everything to a charity she doesn't even support anymore.
Nobody had reviewed it since. The thing was her godson: she's watched him grow up,
never thought she was 'allowed' to include him properly. Now he's in there, the right
charity's in there, and it finally looks like *her* life. Check your will's not a
stranger's. Another family, sorted."

**EP-23 — The wedding fund with a condition**
"Had a laugh today. Grandad setting up a wedding fund for his granddaughter — with
one written condition: the band has to play one Elvis song, for him, whether he's
there or not. We made it work. His daughter rang me after to say the whole family's
crying and laughing about it. That's what this job is on a good day. Another family,
sorted."

**EP-24 — The digital shoebox**
"Something modern today. Client asked, 'What happens to my photos?' Thirty thousand
pictures on a phone, accounts nobody can open. So the plan now covers the digital
side — who gets access, where the passwords live, and a printed album of the best
two hundred for each child, sorted while she's here to caption them. Your photos are
part of your estate. Nobody thinks about it. Another family, sorted."

**EP-25 — The equaliser**
"Delicate one today, handled well. Parents helped one son buy a house years ago;
the other never needed it — but it's quietly bugged everyone since. The thing was
just writing it down: the plan now evens it up, openly, with a note explaining the
thinking. Two brothers who might have fallen out one day now never will. Ten minutes
of honesty on paper. Another family, sorted."

**EP-26 — The library**
"Lovely detail in today's meeting. A retired professor with two thousand books, and
his family were dreading 'the skip'. So the plan sends the collection where it's
loved — the rare ones to his old college, the rest to the little library he's used
for fifty years, with a bookplate in each one with his name. He said, 'I'll be on
someone's shelf forever.' Another family, sorted."

**EP-27 — Christmas, protected**
"Today's thing was Christmas. A mum of four, recently diagnosed, unbelievably brave,
and what she wanted from her estate plan wasn't complicated: Christmas stays at the
family house, there's a fund for it, and her eldest holds the recipe for the red
cabbage. We did all the serious documents too — but that's not what she came for.
She came to protect Christmas. And now it is. Another family, sorted."

**EP-28 — The apology**
"Heavy one today, shared with permission because it matters. A father estranged from
his daughter for fifteen years. His will used to just… leave her out. The thing was a
letter — we include it with his documents — that says the things he's never managed
to say, whatever happens between them. He said writing it was the hardest and best
thing he's done in years. Estate planning is sometimes just the truth, filed
properly. Another family, on the way to sorted."

**EP-29 — The 'boring' clients**
"Funny one today. A couple apologised for being 'a boring case' — normal house,
normal savings, two kids. Sorted the lot in ninety minutes: wills, powers of
attorney, guardians for the kids, the works. The thing was her face when I said
'that's it, you're done, you never have to think about this again unless life
changes.' Boring is the goal. Boring means everyone's okay. Another family, sorted."

**EP-30 — The guardianship conversation**
"Today's meeting was two young parents, and one question they'd been avoiding for
five years: who takes the kids if the worst happens? Nobody wants to pick. We talked
it through, they chose, we wrote it down — and the thing was the exhale afterwards.
Five years of a background worry, gone in an hour. If you've got young kids and no
guardianship in place, that's the one to sort first. Another family, sorted."

## 3. IFA scripts (12)

> Same formula, financial-planning flavour. **Every IFA clip goes through compliance
> sign-off before posting** — no return promises, no specific product pushes, no
> figures presented as typical.

**IFA-01 — The pension drawer full of letters**
"Hey, just wanted to share today's meeting. New client with a drawer — an actual
drawer — of pension letters from five old jobs. Nobody had ever sat down and made
sense of it with her. We can sort all of that, that's the job. But the thing was: once
she could see it all in one place, the fear went. She's not behind. She's actually
okay. She just couldn't see it before. Sometimes the plan *is* the peace of mind.
Another family, sorted."

**IFA-02 — Retire three years earlier**
"Okay, this was a good day. Ran a full cashflow plan for a couple who assumed they
were working until 67 — because nobody had ever actually done the maths with them.
The thing was watching the graph land and him going quiet and saying, 'So I could see
more of my grandkids while they still think I'm fun?' That's what the spreadsheet is
*for*. Another family, sorted."

**IFA-03 — Permission to spend**
"Today's thing wasn't saving money — it was spending it. A widow who's been living
carefully for years, frightened of running out. The plan showed her, clearly, what
she can safely enjoy. First thing she did? Booked the trip to see her sister in
Australia. Some people need a plan to grow money. Some need a plan for permission.
Another family, sorted."

**IFA-04 — The swimming lessons**
"Small one, lovely one. Grandparents wanted to help with the grandkids but didn't
know how to do it sensibly. We walked through the gifting allowances they'd never
used — and now the swimming lessons, all three kids, are quietly covered every year.
The thing is how *normal* it felt by the end. Helping your family well is a skill,
and it's learnable. Another family, sorted."

**IFA-05 — The life cover nobody understood**
"Today: a couple paying for three overlapping life policies sold to them over the
years — couldn't tell you what any of them did. Fresh pair of eyes, tidied it into
cover that actually fits their family, properly set up in trust. The thing was him
saying, 'You're the first person who's explained this in English.' That's the bar,
apparently. Happy to clear it. Another family, sorted."

**IFA-06 — The quarterly meal, IFA edition**
"Just wanted to share this one. Reviewing a client's plans, we found income they
didn't realise they had headroom on. They didn't want a new kitchen or a car. So
it now pays for one thing: a standing family lunch, every quarter, everyone invited,
grandad's treat. Bringing the family together on a schedule, out of money that was
just sitting there. Best use of a review meeting I've seen all year. Another family,
sorted."

**IFA-07 — The daughter's business**
"Lovely meeting today. Client's daughter has been building a little business for two
years, working nights. The plan review showed they could back her — properly,
sensibly, without risking their own retirement. The thing was his phone call to her
from our car park. I could hear her reaction from where I was standing. Another
family, sorted."

**IFA-08 — The single mum's safety net**
"Today mattered. A single mum, two kids, told me she lies awake wondering what
happens to them if something happens to her. We built the safety net: the right
protection, a will referral, guardianship, an emergency fund plan. The thing was her
saying, 'So they'd be okay. Actually okay.' Yes. That's the whole point of all of
it. Another family, sorted."

**IFA-09 — The couple who'd never talked about money**
"Interesting one today. Married thirty-one years — never once talked about money
together. Separate accounts, separate worries. Our first meeting was the first time
they'd seen the whole picture in one place. The thing was they held hands halfway
through. A financial plan is sometimes just a conversation two people needed a
referee for. Another family, sorted."

**IFA-10 — The inheritance that felt like a burden**
"Today's client inherited from her mum last year and hasn't touched it — it felt
wrong to. We talked about what her mum would have wanted, and built the plan around
that: some security, some future, and one thing that's pure joy — the garden studio
she's always wanted, to paint in. She said, 'Mum would love that.' The thing was
turning grief-money back into love-money. Another family, sorted."

**IFA-11 — The 'too late' myth**
"Quick myth-bust from today's meeting. Client, 68, convinced it was 'too late' for
planning to matter — nearly didn't come in. An hour later: pensions organised, tax
allowances actually being used, a gifting plan for the grandkids. The thing was him
asking, 'Why did I think it was too late?' Because nobody told you otherwise. It's
almost never too late to get sorted. Another family, sorted."

**IFA-12 — The handover between generations**
"Big one today. Three generations in one room — grandparents, their son, his kids —
doing something almost nobody does: talking about the family money, together, on
purpose. No secrets, no surprises later. The thing was the grandson, 19, asking
smart questions and grandad beaming. That family will never have a 'reading of the
will' shock. That's the goal. One family — three generations — sorted."

## 4. How to generate infinite new scripts

The bank above seeds the first ~3 months. After that, stories come from real weeks.
Adviser prompt (put this on a card / in the team channel):

> Think about this week's meetings. Which client moment would you tell your partner
> about over dinner? That's the video. Say it exactly like you'd say it at dinner,
> starting with "I just wanted to share…", pivot on "but that's not the thing — the
> thing was…", and end with "Another family, sorted."

Weekly content call (15 min, Monday): each adviser says their "thing" from last week
in one sentence; the content lead picks angles, flags any two that are too similar,
and checks anonymisation. Then everyone films Tuesday–Thursday.
